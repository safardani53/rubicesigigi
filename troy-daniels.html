    College Career

    Early Years 

    Troy joined the Charlotte Bobcats for the 2013 NBA Summer League after going undrafted in the 2013 NBA draft.

    Troy scored 32 points and nailed ten three-pointers against the Idaho Stampede on January 4, 2014.

    Likewise, he also shattered the D- League’s single-season three-pointer record halfway through his debut season with the Vipers. 

    Even though Daniels signed with the Houston Rockets on February 21, 2014, but was promptly sent back to the Vipers.

    The player was later sent to the Vipers again. He notched 21.8 points, 4.5 rebounds, 1.8 assists, and 1.1 steals per game in 50 games with the Vipers in 2013–14.

    Troy made the game-winning three-point jumper with 11.0 seconds remaining in overtime against the Portland Trail Blazers in Game 3 of their 2014 NBA Playoffs first-round matchup, which the Rockets won 121–116.

    He had 17 points at the end of the game, but the Rockets went on to lose the series 4–2. Then, Troy re-signed with the Rockets on July 14, 2014.

    EuroLeague

    On July 12, 2021, he relocated to Europe after signing a one-year contract with the Italian team Olimpia Milano, coached by Ettore Messina.

    According to him, it may not be as quick, but it can be rapid at times, and the physicality transforms the game.

    “In the NBA, touching a player may result in a foul, but in the Euroleague, you can push about and do anything you want, up to a point. It has the quirk to revolutionize the game.”

    That was something the player got to experience throughout his first two or three weeks.

    It was difficult for him at the beginning. However, he said that it made the game more enjoyable once you’ve gotten used to it.

    He further added that it counted to the game’s significance because every possession and competition mattered.
