    TOP Chef host Padma Lakshmi has been spotted for the first time since quitting the show.

    Padma, 52, had not been seen since before last week when she shocked Bravo fans with the announcement that she was leaving the reality show after almost 20 years.

    The former Top Chef host was spotted on Monday arriving at an office building in Manhattan, New York.

    Padma sported a business casual look while wearing black pants, a super low-cut black shirt, and a striped white jacket over it.

    She had her hair down while wearing a necklace and black shoes.

    Earlier this week, she took to social media to post a statement regarding her hosting duty.

    PADMA'S FAREWELL

    She wrote: "After much soul searching, I have made the difficult decision to leave Top Chef."

    The host continued: "Having completed a glorious 20th season as host and executive producer, I am extremely proud to have been part of building such a successful show and the impact it has had in the worlds of television and food.

    "After 17 years, many of the cast and crew are like family to me and I will miss working alongside them dearly."

    She revealed that her career is now moving in a new direction, writing: "I feel it's time to move on and need to make space for Taste the Nation, my books, and other creative pursuits."

    The TV personality concluded by sharing her appreciation for all of her fans: "I am deeply thankful to all of you for so many years of love and support."

    The Tangy, Tart, Hot and Sweet author signed off by simply writing: "Sincerely, Padma."

    'YOU ARE TOP CHEF'

    In the post's comment section, fans thanked the longtime host for entertaining them over the 20 seasons she spent on Top Chef and wished her well in her future endeavors.

    One person wrote: "Thanks for 17 stellar years! We are glad you got to pack your knives and go on your own terms. Looking forward to more wonderful creative endeavors from you, Padma!"

    A second fan commented: "Oh no, it won't be the same but wish you good luck with future endeavors."

    "That’s it. That’s the last season I will watch," announced a third unhappy commenter, as another added: "I’m distraught."

    A fourth fan begged: "Please don’t pack your knives and go."

    While a fifth stated: "You are Top Chef."

    MORE ABOUT PADMA

    Padma and her co-host Tom Colicchio, 60, are food masters in the cooking industry and have both won Emmy awards for their co-hosting duties in the cooking competition.

    Padma is a food expert, TV host, producer, and a New York Times best-selling author, who often regards herself as a home cook and not a chef.

    Aside from hosting and executive producing Top Chef, she also hosts, produces, and created the critically acclaimed Hulu series, Taste the Nation.

    The cook was born in India but was raised in the US and started her career as a fashion model and actress in both Europe and America.

    She is the co-founder of the Endometriosis Foundation of America (EFA) and an American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) Artist Ambassador for immigrants’ rights and women’s rights. 

    Padma was also appointed a Goodwill Ambassador for the United Nations Development Program (UNDP).

    MORE ON TOP CHEF

    Like Padma, co-host Tom is well accomplished and is a top chef known widely in New York and around the world.

    He hails from New Jersey and is of Italian descent, which is often reflected in his culinary recipes.

    Tom is a restaurateur and founded Craft Hospitality, which currently includes New York's Craft, Riverpark, Temple Court, and Small Batch; Los Angeles' Craft Los Angeles; and Las Vegas' Heritage Steak and Craftsteak.

    He is the recipient of five James Beard Foundation Awards for cooking accomplishments through his various authored books.

    The chef serves on the boards of Children of Bellevue, City Harvest, and Wholesome Wave.

    Gail Simmons has been a recurring judge on Top Chef since its inception back in March 2006.

    The 46-year-old is a Canadian-American food writer, cookbook author, and mother of two children.

    She was previously absent from Season 16 due to her second pregnancy.

    When asked by Entertainment Weekly in March 2023 if she thinks there will be another 20 seasons of Top Chef, Simmons responded: "God willing. We'll all be in walkers, but we'll do it happily! You know, who knows?
