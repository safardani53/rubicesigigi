    Ernesto Zedillo Ponce de León CYC GColIH GCMG is a Mexican economist and politician. He was President of Mexico from December 1, 1994 to November 30, 2000, the last on a list of 71 years without interruption for Mexican presidents of the Institutional Revolutionary Party. During his presidency, he faced one of the worst economic crises in Mexican history, which began just weeks after taking office. He left his predecessor Carlos Salinas de Gortari, blamed his administration for the crisis and oversaw the arrest of his brother Raúl Salinas de Gortari. His administration was also marked, among other things, by new clashes with the EZLN and the People's Revolutionary Army; controversial implementation of Fobaproa to bail out the national banking system; a political reform that allowed residents of the federal county to elect their own mayors; and the massacres of Aguas Blancas and Acteal by state forces.
