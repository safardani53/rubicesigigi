    MANCHESTER City and England star Raheem Sterling has taken his former home off the market after failing to find a buyer.

    The 22-year-old star put the four-bedroom mansion up for sale after moving from former club Liverpool to City last summer.

    The house, in Birkdale, Merseyside, went on the market for £1.5m in August 2015 but the price was slashed to £1.2m - £50k less than he paid - in March.

    The property’s listing has now been removed from the Rightmove website, despite land registry records showing the home has not been sold since Sterling bought it September 2013.

    The detached home includes an indoor swimming pool and jacuzzi, a barber's salon, home cinema and even a Michael Jackson-themed bar area complete with a moon-walking pair of legs hanging from the ceiling.

    A heated swimming pool has been installed inside

    More Reds memorabilia is in the home cinema room

    The garden is equipped with two football goals

    The master bedroom is decorated with splashes of zebra print while a black and white theme dominates most areas of the luxurious home including the kitchen.

    Sterling has turned one of the rooms into a personal gym and two framed Liverpool shirts adorn the walls of the home cinema room, which comes with nine red leather reclining seats.

    The Michael Jackson room has MJ memorabilia hung on the walls, its own bar, a DJ deck set up and blue lighting in the skirting-boards.

    In addition, the property is surrounded by landscape gardens, currently equipped two football goals and a basketball court.

    It’s understood Sterling had wanted a quick sale after moving to his new home in Cheshire but he may now have to consider renting out the property, which is situated in an area popular with current and former football stars.

    The England international has recently revealed he is expecting a baby boy with girlfriend Paige Milian.

    Only last week was Sterling the hero, after his sumptuous strike against Arsenal earned all three points for Pep Guardiola's men.

    He started off the season in blistering form, scoring five times in the opening ten games of the campaign, but the goals dried up, leaving his manager to issue him a stern warning.

    Manchester City sit in third place in the Premier League, seven points behind leaders Chelsea as the Citizens travel to KC Stadium on Boxing Day to take on Hull City.

    Then on New Year's Eve they face Jurgen Klopp's Liverpool away from home - the last time they came out victorious against the Reds at Anfield was back in 2003.
