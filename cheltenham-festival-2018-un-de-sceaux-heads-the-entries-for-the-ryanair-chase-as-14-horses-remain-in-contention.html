    UN DE SCEAUX heads a field of 14 horses left in contention for the Ryanair Chase at Cheltenham.

    Willie Mullins' chaser won the race last year and is the favourite to successfully defend his crown at Prestbury Park.

    Mullins has also left in Bachasson, Djakadam, Douvan, Min, Yorkhill and Benie Des Dieux.

    The potential Irish challenge is completed by Henry de Bromhead's pair of Sub Lieutenant and Balko Des Flos.

    Latest Cheltenham Festival news

    Ruth Jefferson has decided against running stable star Waiting Patiently, but is still set to be represented by the ultra-consistent Cloudy Dream.

    Jefferson said: "He's absolutely bouncing at the moment. He'd probably prefer better ground, but he does have form with cut in the ground.

    "None of us know what the ground is going to be like at this stage. There's some rain forecast over the weekend and then a few showers at the start of the next week, so we'll just have to wait and see which way it goes.
