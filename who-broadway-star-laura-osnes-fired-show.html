    Who is Broadway star Laura Osnes and was she fired from a show?

    Laura Osnes is an American actress.

    She has been performing on Broadway since 2008, starring in South Pacific, Bonnie & Clyde, Carousel, Bandstand, and others.

    The actress has also starred in Hallmark movies and the HBO series Six by Sondheim.

    Osnes was fired from the latest Broadway production after she allegedly refused to get vaccinated for coronavirus. 

    The news about the 35-year-old performer was initially reported by Page Six.

    She was originally set to appear in a one-night production of Crazy For You at the Guild Hall in East Hampton later this month.

    Theater staff and performers must either be jabbed or present a negative coronavirus test result, the Guild Hall told Page Six.

    Sources reportedly said the matter was brought up by her co-star Tony Yazbeck, who has two children.  

    After this, she was said to have been let go from the cast. 

    What did Broadway say about Laura Osnes?

    The theater's artistic director Josh Gladstone spoke to Page Six in light of Osnes’ news, saying: “We have a requirement now along the lines of what Actors' Equity is requiring, and what Broadway is requiring, for performances. 

    “So yes — we're very excited with the cast that we have, and we're delighted Susan has put together a beautiful evening. 

    “We're sorry not to have Laura on this, [and] we will look forward to working with Laura again. 

    “We are concerned about maintaining the safety of our staff and our audiences.”   

    Who is Laura Osnes married to?

    Osnes has been married to professional photographer Nathan Johnson.

    The pair met in 2003 during a production of Disney’s Aladdin and married four years later.
