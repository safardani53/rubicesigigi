    The gentle and dynamic Ryan Kelly is an American-born professional basketball player. He currently plays as a power forward for the Sun Rockers Shibuya in the Japanese B. League.

    However, Kelly began his professional league journey in the NBA by playing for the Los Angeles Lakers in 2013. After failing to deliver in the NBA, he went abroad to try in foreign leagues just after four seasons.

    Before his NBA debut, he played college basketball for the Duke Blue Devils until 2013. He won the NCAA championship with the Devils in 2010.

    The former NBA power forward is not the top player but always strives to give his best while appearing on the court. This spirit improved his game in his college years, which saved him from getting undrafted in the 2013 NBA draft.

    But sadly, the exact opposite happened with him in the NBA. His performance went downhill in the second season and kept declining until he exited early in 2017.

    Ryan Kelly | Early Life, Family & Education

    On April 9, 1991, Ryan Kelly was born in Caramel, New York, United States. He was the first child of his parents, Chris and Doreen Kelly. He has a younger brother named Sean and a younger sister named Erin in his family.

    Even though Ryan was a native of Caramel, he initially attended the local school in Caramel. However, in the late nineties, he shifted to the Ravenscroft School in Raleigh, North Carolina.

    His mother, Doreen, joined the teaching faculty in 1999, and all his siblings later attended the same school. His mother, Doreen, has been serving as the head of school at Ravenscroft since 2003.

    Besides being an educated family, Ryan’s parents were very enthusiastic about sports. His mother, Doreen, was a former volleyball player at the University of Pennsylvania.

    Similarly, his father, Chris, was a former professional basketball player who played for around three seasons in the French league. So, he had an early motivation to take part in sports events.

    Interestingly, his brother, Sean, was just two years younger than him, and he liked to play with his brother. So, they grew up playing basketball together. His father, Chris, played along and helped his young boys hone their basketball skills.

    Furthermore, he kept improving as he got more match exposure playing the AAU tournaments with the D-One Sports team.

    Being born into an affluent family, Ryan got everything he needed from his parents. Besides basketball, he was into all the stuff that 90s kids liked doing, such as playing video games and watching the popular American sitcom Seinfeld.

    Kelly eventually graduated his high school in 2009 and joined Duke University for higher education. His younger brother attended the same university and played college basketball for the Devils.

    Ryan Kelly | Basketball Career

    High School Career

    Ryan played his four-year high school basketball for the Ravenscroft School in Raleigh, North Carolina. Kelly started as a power forward in his first season, and he was extremely good at it.

    He finished his sophomore season with an average of 14.2 points and 8.7 rebounds per game. Even though Ryan had pretty decent numbers that season, he was not satisfied, so he geared up in his last two seasons.

    Consequently, he averaged around 25.0 and 10.0 rebounds per game in his junior and senior seasons. For his performance, he was selected for the McDonald’s All-American and Jordan Brand All-American games in 2009.

    Also, popular sports agency Scouts.com recognized him as one of the top recruits in the country. He left his high school keeping many records, including all-time best scorer and rebounds.

    College Career

    In 2009, Ryan Kelly enrolled at Duke University to play college basketball. Consequently, the legendary college basketball coach Mike Krzyzewski took an interest in him and immediately liked his style of play.

    However, the Duke Blue Devils had its capable power forward, Kyle Singler, so he mainly played as a substitute for his senior. As he had less playtime, he could only average 1.2 points per game as a freshman.

    The following year, Kelly got more opportunities and somehow improved his game. The team management deemed him capable of leading the Devils, so he was made the team captain in his last two college seasons.

    Although he had far better performances in the last two seasons, he missed many games due to a foot injury. He averaged over 10.0 points and 5.0 rebounds in his final college seasons.

    Professional Career

    The Los Angeles Lakers selected Kelly as the 2013 NBA draft’s 48th overall pick in the second round. Consequently, Ryan signed a rookie scale contract with the Lakers on September 20, 2013.

    He appeared in 59 matches in his rookie season and finished it with an average of 8.0 points and 3.7 rebounds per game. In addition, the Lakers sent him to play in the NBA G League for their affiliate club, Los Angeles D-Fenders as well.

    They became impressed with his rookie season performance and re-signed him for another two years on July 21, 2014. But, contrary to their expectation, Kelly’s performance was miserable even though they allowed him more playtime in the second season.

    After another bad season, he went to the Atlanta Hawks for the 2016-17 season. He only played nine matches for the Hawks before playing for the Maine Red Claws in the NBA G League.

    Foreign Leagues

    Kelly joined the Spanish club, Real Betis Energia Plus, on September 12, 2017, after spending a brief time with the Houston Rockets. He played one season of the Liga ACB before moving to Japan to play in the B. League.

    Since 2018, he has been regularly playing for the Tokyo club, Sun Rockers Shibuya, in the top basketball league in Japan.

    Besides his club career, Ryan Kelly has represented the United States national team in different international tournaments. Ryan was part of the American team that lifted the FIBA Americas U-18 Championship trophy in 2008.

    Ryan Kelly | Net Worth & Career Earnings

    One of the best American players to play overseas leagues, Ryan Kelly, has a net worth somewhere between $1 million to $5 million. His basic source of earning is the league games.

    After getting picked up by the Los Angeles Lakers, he signed a two-year contract worth $1.5 million. The Lakers paid him around $490k for his rookie season alone. They offered him a considerable sum of $1.65 million for his second season.

    Similarly, the Lakers increased his salary to $1.72 million when he appeared in the third season for them. 

    Then, the Atlanta Hawks acquired him by signing a contract worth around $1 million in 2016, but they released him soon after appearing in six preseason games.

    However, he later rejoined them and played nine more 2016-17 season games. Overall, he earned around $713k from his games for the Atlanta Hawks.

    Summing up, Ryan accumulated around $4.5 million from his overall NBA career.

    Ryan is currently playing for the Sun Rockers Shibuya and earning pretty well. However, he has not revealed his exact earnings to the public. But seeing his comfortable lifestyle in Japan, one can rest assured that he receives a fair annual salary.
