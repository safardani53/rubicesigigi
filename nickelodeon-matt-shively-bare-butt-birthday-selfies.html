    OFF SCREEN

    Meanwhile, one top Nickelodeon TV star spoke about trading their dreams of stardom for a different career when they ran out of acting work.

    Former child star Devon Werkheiser, 32, starred as the protagonist in hit Nickelodeon series Ned's Declassified.

    The show debuted in 2004 and was three seasons long.

    It followed main character Ned Bigby, played by Devon, who offered advice for viewers about how to survive school, all while trying to figure things out for himself.

    Unfortunately as he revealed, his career seemed to stagnate when the show wrapped in 2007.

    Despite seeking the help of an acting coach, he never saw his career take off beyond a few other, smaller roles.

    He told Business Insider that, aged 25, he needed a 9-5 job.

    The star explained: "I was never ridiculous with my money, but I would eat out or go on trips when I wanted to.

    "I'd always be living on my savings until the next job came and refilled it, then I'd live on my savings some more.

    "Over the years, supporting-role jobs started paying less in the industry, and at the same time I was booking less and less."
