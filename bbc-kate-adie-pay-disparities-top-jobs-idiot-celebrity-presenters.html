    VETERAN BBC war reporter Kate Adie has blamed huge pay disparities on bosses giving top jobs to “idiot” celebrity presenters.

    The Beeb’s former chief news correspondent, 71, described the huge salaries of some the Beeb’s highest paid stars, which were published last week, as “an extraordinary inflation of money”.

    The report revealed that Radio 2 breakfast show host Chris Evans, 51, earned more than £2.2million and Match of the Day frontman Gary Lineker, 56, was paid more than £1.75m.

    The highest-paid woman, Claudia Winkleman, 45, received more than £450,000.

    But Kate, who now presents Radio 4’s From Our Own Correspondent, and assignments have included the Gulf Wars and the war in the Balkans, thinks the spending has got out of hand.

    She said: “One problem is this kind of celebrity presenter.

    "It’s all about celebrity now. It’s about fame, glamour, publicity, celebrity — it’s the extraordinary inflation of money for success, like footballers.

    "Idiots who have absolutely nothing make millions.

    MOST READ IN BIZARRE

    “I find attaching a name to everything trivial, the most interesting people I’ve met are not famous.”

    Now the BBC is facing a growing clamour over equal pay after more than 40 female broadcasters have called on Tony Hall, the director-general, to take urgent action to tackle the gender pay gap.

    But Kate, who wasn’t on the full list of stars earning more than £150,000, defended former Top Gear host Chris, despite him topping the pay list.

    She added: “Top salaries come about for people who’ve been in the business for a long time and who’s a real pro.

    Chris knows the business and he’s a hard worker. He’s the real deal.”

    Meanwhile, Mary Poppins actress Julie Walters has spoken out about gender pay inequality in the film industry.

    In the latest issue of Good Housekeeping (must keep) she said: “It’s about equality of opportunity.

    "It’s good that we’re talking about it. It should be out there.

    "A lot of people don’t know that it is the case. The men were earning more money. Why? It’s the same bloody job!

    “It’s women who go to the cinema more generally speaking, so it doesn’t make sense from that point of view either.

    "I think it’s wrong. Equal pay is the answer.”
