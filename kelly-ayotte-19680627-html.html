    Kelly Ann Ayotte is an American lawyer and politician who served as a United States Senator from 2011 to 2017 and Attorney General of New Hampshire from 2004 to 2009. Born and raised in Nashua, New Hampshire, Ayotte is a graduate of Nashua High School, Pennsylvania State University and Villanova. Faculty of University Law. She worked as a law clerk at the New Hampshire Supreme Court before entering private practice. She was also a prosecutor for the New Hampshire Department of Justice and briefly legal counsel to Governor Craig Benson of New Hampshire, before returning to the Department of Justice to serve as Deputy Attorney General of New Hampshire. In June 2004, Governor Benson appointed Ayotte Attorney General for New Hampshire, following the resignation of Peter Heed. She became the first and only woman to serve as New Hampshire's attorney general, from 2004 to 2009, after being re-appointed twice by Democratic Governor John Lynch.
