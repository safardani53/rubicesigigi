    Sideline Reporting Career 

    Before becoming professional, Jamie opted for an internship at Comcast Sports Net in Washington, wherein she would host a 30-minute magazine show.

    After that, with a certified degree, she was recruited to New England Sports Network (NESN) late.

    Her job was joint hosting and reporting. In New England, the channel was quite popular among fans, and the hosts, Adam Pellerin and Erdahl, came in high demand.

    The Minnesotan filled in for Jenny Dell to cover Boston Red Sox’s games in 2013. Dell had to drop from hosting duties due to personal reasons. This was the exact moment when Jamie made an impact with her coverage.

    Subsequently, Erdahl received a promotion to rinkside and, at the same time, juggled her time between studios and sidelines. Jamie’s main task at the studio was hosting a 30-minute news program.

    According to records, she was a freelance reporter for FOX Sports, for whom she covered events about the WNBA team Minnesota Lynx for a live telecast. A few years of experience at NESN opened the door to CBS Sports.

    To this date, Jamie is an employee at CBS and assists Greg Gumbel and Trent Green with her valuable coverage and research. After the firing of Allie LaForce in late 2018, Erdahl became a leading on-site journalist, mainly for CBS Sports’ affiliate SEC.

    She joined NFL Network’s Good Morning Football (GMFB) as co-host in 2022.

    On 7 January 2023, she took an Instagram to educate people about CPR training. Moreover, she thanked the American Heart Association for its incredible job in spreading awareness.
