    Margot Robbie's Barbie beats Oppenheimer in Golden Globe nominations as British stars celebrate

    BARBIE defeated Oppenheimer at the box office battle and now she is set to win out in awards season too. The hit film starring Margot Robbie as the title character has landed nine nominations for the Golden Globes while Oppenheimer got eight.

    08 May 2024, 00:00

    BARBIE defeated Oppenheimer at the box office battle and now she is set to win out in awards season too.

    The hit film starring Margot Robbie as the title character has landed nine nominations for the Golden Globes while Oppenheimer got eight.

    It becomes the second most-nominated film ever, drawing with 1972’s Cabaret and second to 1975’s Nashville, which got 11 nods.

    The nominations, announced today, have the movie up for Best Film (Musical or Comedy), Best Director for Greta Gerwig, Best Screenplay and Cinematic And Box Office Achievement, in which it will compete against Oppenheimer.

    Margot is nominated for Best Actress (Musical or Comedy) and Ryan Gosling, who played her boyfriend Ken, is up for Best Supporting Actor.

    The soundtrack heralded three more nods, for Dua Lipa’s Dance The Night, Ryan’s I’m Just Ken and Billie Eilish’s What Was I Made For?.

    Voting scandal

    Dua said: “Wow waking up a Golden Globe nomineeeeeee!!! This barbie is so grateful!

    “So happy I get to share this moment with my collaborators for our song Dance The Night Caroline Ailin, Mark Ronson and Andrew Wyatt.

    “So much love to the Greta, Noah (Baumbach, writer), Margot and Ryan on their much deserved nominations!!! Let’s go Barbie!”

    Margot faces tough competition in her category as she will go up against Jennifer Lawrence for No Hard Feelings, Natalie Portman for May December and Emma Stone for her black comedy Poor Things.

    Oppenheimer is in the running for Best Film - Drama, Best Director, Best Screenplay and Best Original Score, as well as Best Actor - Drama for Cillian Murphy, Best Supporting Actress for British star Emily Blunt and Best Supporting Actor for Robert Downey Jr.

    The annual awards ceremony, which will take place in Los Angeles on January 7, celebrates the best in film and television

    Pop stars also got a look in, with Taylor Swift’s Eras Tour film landing a spot in the Cinematic and Box Office Achievement category, while her pal Selena Gomez was nominated for her turn in Only Murders In The Building.

    The Golden Globes are attempting to reinvent themselves after a series of controversies, particularly a 2021 scandal when it was revealed that out of 91 members on its voting panel, none of them were black.

    The membership has now been increased to 300 people from 75 countries.

    SUCCESSION AT TOP OF TV PACK

    SUCCESSION rules the roost in the television categories with nine nominations.

    It is way ahead of its nearest competition, comedy-drama The Bear about an award-winning chef, which has five nods.

    Succession is up for Best TV Series (Drama) and its cast have been recognised in individual acting categories.

    Brian Cox, Kieran Culkin and Jeremy Strong make up three of the six nominees for Best Actor in a TV Series (Drama).

    Half of the nominees in the Best Supporting Actor category also come from the series, with Alan Ruck, Alexander Skarsgard and Brit Matthew Macfadyen being recognised.

    Sarah Snook is in the Best Actress in a TV Series (Drama) and J. Smith Cameron is up for Best Supporting Actress.

    Succession, about the dramas of the Roy family who own a global media and entertainment empire called Waystar RoyCo, ended with its fourth series in May.

    The Crown received four nominations including in the Best Television Series (Drama) category, where it will compete against 1923, The Diplomat, The Last Of Us, The Morning Show and Succession.

    Delighted Ricky leads British charge

    RICKY Gervais, Gary Oldman and Hannah Waddingham are among the Brits celebrating nominations.

    The funnyman who has hosted the ceremony five times, was recognised for his special Armageddon in the Best Stand-Up Comedian on Television category, ahead of its release later this month.

    He wrote on X: “Record-breaking tour, on Netflix Christmas Day, Golden Globe Nomination. What a cracking f***ing year. Cheers.”

    Hannah nominated for Best Supporting Actress in the football comedy Ted Lasso, wrote: “Whaaaaaaaat the fuuuuuuuuu! I’ve died and gone to heaven!”

    Gary is in the Best Actor in a TV Series (Drama) category opposite fellow Brit Dominic West, from The Crown.

    Imelda Staunton landed a Best Actress nomination for the same show, her first nod since 2005.

    Rosamund Pike, Rachel Weisz, Andrew Scott and composer Daniel Pemberton were among other Brits who were recognised.

    Ireland had reason to cheer too, with Cork’s Cillian Murphy going up against Dublin’s Barry Keoghan, from the movie Saltburn, for the Best Actor (Drama) gong.

    Will they join the Euro club?

    BLUE had a good crack at Eurovision when they took part in 2011 and now fellow Noughties group S Club could try to win it.

    Jon Lee from the group has insisted he is well up for the challenge, even if his bandmates would need some persuading.

    During an exclusive chat at Sunday’s Capital Jingle Bell Ball in London, he said: “It would be amazing. I don’t think it can get much camper than that. Eurovision would be amazing.”

    But Jo O’Meara said: “You know what, I don’t think I’d have the bottle.”

    And Tina Barrett added: “One thing I will say, they don’t like the UK, do they?

    “They always give nul points. There would be more glitter if we did it.”

    For now the band are focusing on returning with more new music after dropping their first song in two decades, These Are The Days, in July.

    Jon added: “We’ll definitely do some new music.

    “We’re going to concentrate mainly on doing some live shows next year, maybe like the Isle of Wight Festival and a few things like that.

    “Then a few more international tours and stuff so lots happening.”

    CARDI: MY RAP MARRIAGE OVER

    CARDI B has confirmed the end of her marriage to rapper Offset after six years.

    The WAP hitmaker spoke out after days of speculation that he had yet another affair behind her back.

    Cardi told fans via Instagram Live: “I don’t know if you have been getting clues from me from my lives, my stories, who I follow and when I put certain music on my page.

    “I have been single for a minute and I have been afraid to...not afraid, but I haven’t known how to tell the world.

    “It has been like this for a minute now. I want to start 2024 fresh and open.

    “I am curious for a new life for a new beginning and I’m excited.”

    She added of the cheating allegations: “I don’t think it’s true but I don’t care to find out.”

    The couple have two children but it’s not been a straightforward relationship.

    Cardi filed for divorce in September 2020 but they later rekindled their romance.

    In September last year, on their fifth wedding anniversary, she said she was planning a full wedding ceremony, having originally got married in secret.

    Well at least she saved the money on throwing that.

    Her second album, a follow-up to 2018’s Invasion Of Privacy, has been a long-time coming and I’ve no doubt this will inspire plenty more tracks.

    Nicki heads over for gigs

    NICKI Minaj will visit the UK on tour next year after announcing a trio of dates following the release of her new album.

    The rapper will kick things off at Manchester’s new arena Co-Op Live on May 25, then play Birmingham’s Resorts World Arena on May 26 and London’s O2 Arena on May 28.

    Tickets will go on sale through livenation.co.uk on Friday this week.

    She released her album Pink Friday 2 last week and it is set to go to No2 in the charts, being beaten by The Killers’ new hits collection Rebel Diamonds.

    Colombian singer-songwriter Karol G, who Nicki collaborated with on 2019 song Tusa, has also just announced a European tour with a UK stop at London’s O2 Arena on June 18.
