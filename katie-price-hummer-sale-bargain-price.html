    KATIE Price's new £60,000 Hummer has been put up for sale at a "bargain" price after she was slammed for driving it.

    The former glamour model was spotted getting behind the wheel of the three-tonne monster motor wearing flimsy slip-on backless shoes, just days after getting her licence back.

    Katie, 44, had been banned from driving following a booze and cocaine-fuelled car crash until earlier this month.

    But she looked to have splashed out on a £60,000 motor, with a personalised number plate, after her two-year ban was lifted.

    After she was met with backlash from road safety campaigners yesterday for driving the car in sliders, the motor is on sale.

    Despite just days behind the wheel, detailed photos of the Hummer have been now posted online on a car trading site.

    The vehicle - detailed as a six-seater SUV registered in 2008 - is now available to buy on car site Auto Trader.

    Katie's £60,000 car is currently listed for a 'bargain' price of £28,995.

    It comes after Katie was slammed by road safety campaigners after driving her new Hummer in a pair of sliders.

    She was spotted in the 15-foot long, 7ft wide American car, which has enormous tyres, near her “Mucky Mansion” home on Sunday.

    Katie was only allowed back on the road on Friday and was all smiles as she was pictured stepping out of the giant car.

    John Scruby, from the Campaign Against Drink Driving, said of the snaps: "Those are the sorts of shoes you'd expect someone to wear at a swimming pool, not driving a huge car like that.

    “It’s an absolute disgrace and is very dangerous. She’s putting herself and other road users at risk.

    “I don’t see how you’d safely be able to control such a car while wearing those shoes.”

    Meanwhile, questions were being asked as to how the bankrupt celebrity could afford the £60,000 motor which has a personalised number plate.

    Despite her appalling record, her two-year ban after crashing her car while over the drink-drive limit was reduced.

    She was sentenced in December 2021 after flipping the vehicle, and was given a suspended 16-week jail term.

    Judge Amanda Kelly said Price had “one of the worst driving records I have seen”.
