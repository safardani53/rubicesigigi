    Masked Singer 2022 LIVE Ken Jeong insists Hydra is one of the Three Amigos while fans guess Ring

    THE Masked Singer's latest episode saw Lemur's elimination, revealing her to be supermodel Christie Brinkley. Masked Singer aired at 8pm ET, and featured Armadillo, Ringmaster, Miss Teddy, Lemur and Hydra as part of Round 2.

    15 Aug 2024, 00:00

    THE Masked Singer's latest episode saw Lemur's elimination, revealing her to be supermodel Christie Brinkley.

    Masked Singer aired at 8pm ET, and featured Armadillo, Ringmaster, Miss Teddy, Lemur and Hydra as part of Round 2.

    Judges Ken Jeong and Robin Thicke both guessed that Hydra might be the Three Amigos: Steve Martin, Martin Short, and Chevy Chase.

    Because he's been "dying" to have Martin Short on the show and because he knows Chase from working with him on Community, Jeong thinks this to be the case.

    Meanwhile, fans are speculating on Twitter that Ringmaster is Hannah Montana and Young and Hungry alumn Emily Osment.

    The guesses come after two eliminations last week - Cyclops and Thingamabob.

    Cyclops was revealed to be actor Jorge Garcia, and Thingamabob was NFL star Jordan Mailata.

    Jorge is best known for playing Hugo "Hurley" Reyes, in the show Lost.

    Firefly made it through to the final from Round 1.

    • Contestants must keep voices disguised

      To hide the person speaking, voices are heavily changed. Even when speaking with the host, they must keep their discussions and exchanges brief, reports Cheatsheet.

      This is done to keep their true identity hidden until the big revelation.

    • Audience members sign NDAs

      Because The Masked Singer is pre-recorded, each audience member must sign a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) vowing not to discuss the program after they leave, according to Cheatsheet.

      This prevents anyone from revealing the plot before the program airs on television.

    • Masked Singers don't get voice lessons

      According to tvovermind.com, contestants on The Masked singer do not receive any vocal training prior to filming.

      Unless singers are already professionally trained, contestants do not otherwise have experience vocally.

    • Newer elements in the series

      In the fourth season, a Golden Ear trophy was introduced, which is granted to the panelist with the most right first impression guesses at the end of the season, as well as a reduction in performances and remote audience voting.

      In the fifth season, these aspects were carried over, while wildcard candidates who appeared at the conclusion of some episodes participated for the first time.

    • How does The Masked Singer work? part three

      Certain performances are not voted on; competitors in an episode may sing as a group at times, and each show ends with the ousted celebrity singing an encore unmasked.

      A smackdown round is included in certain episodes, except in the first and fifth seasons, in which the two least popular contestants from their first performances sing one after the other on the same stage, followed by a second, elimination vote.

      Since the second season, the participants have been separated into subgroups and only compete in one of them.

    • How does The Masked Singer work? continued

      Following the performances, the audience and panelists use an electronic gadget to vote for their favorite performer.

      The show employs a weighted voting method, in which each panelist and audience member’s vote is worth 50 percent of the total score.

      The competitor with the lowest popularity removes their disguise to reveal their true identity.

      This process of elimination continues for a certain number of episodes until only three participants remain in the season finale, after which one is named the champion when they perform once more.

      As a prize, the Golden Mask trophy is presented to the winner.

    • How does The Masked Singer work?

      Each season of The Masked Singer has a different cast of celebrities.

      In an episode, four to six competitors sing a 90-second cover song in costume for panelists and an audience.

      Before and after each performance, hints – or clue packages – about their identities are presented.

      A filmed interview with a celebrity’s electronically disguised voice narrating a video with subtle allusions to what they are renowned for is a recurring format.

      The judges are given the opportunity to guess each singer’s identity out loud and record remarks in note binders during screenings of the clue packages, following performances, and before each elimination.

    • Past Winners: Jewel

      On the December 15, 2021, Masked Singer season 6 finale, Queen of Hearts, unmasked as Jewel, defeated Todrick Hall after winning the Group B finals.

      She subsequently told People that the series allowed her to demonstrate the “technical” part of her voice.

    • Past Winners: Nick Lachey

      The 98 Degrees member won as Piglet and said he went on the program for his children, Camden and Phoenix, as well as Brooklyn, his daughter.

      While the celebrity may have deceived the judges during his time on the program, he claims that his children immediately recognized their father as Piglet.

      “They recognized my voice immediately,” Lachey said to People.

    • Past Winners: LeAnn Rhymes

      The show’s fourth episode came to a close with Grammy winner LeAnn Rimes.

      Rimes, who was masked up as the Sun, afterward expressed how her experience was not what she had expected.

      She said: “I didn’t expect this to be this much work. I have to say. I was thinking like, I’ll come on, this will be easy. This will be fun. And it was a blast.”

    • Past Winners: Kandi Burruss

      As the first female champion of the competition, the Real Housewives of Atlanta star made history.

      Following her win, she said: “For a long time, I really stopped singing by myself because you get negative feedback and so it kind of messes with your head.

      “But thank you. So I had really stopped. And I just really appreciate you guys for helping me build my confidence back.”

    • Past Winners: Wayne Brady

      The former Hamilton performer and Let’s Make a Deal presenter had a hard time keeping his role as the Fox a secret from host Nick Cannon, but after he won, he made sure everyone knew how much fun he had on the show.

      After winning, Brady said: “This has been the coolest, most amazing, most touching, most challenging weirdest, this is the weirdest — and I host Let’s Make a Deal.”

    • Past Winners: T-Pain

      When he won the inaugural season of the competition, the Grammy winner set the tone for all future winners.

      Before winning the Golden Mask, he defeated Gladys Knight and Donny Osmond.

    • Who has previously won The Masked Singer?

      Throughout its six seasons, the following celebrities have won the Golden Mask:

      • Season 1: T-Pain
      • Season 2: Wayne Brady
      • Season 3: Kandi Burruss
      • Season 4: LeAnn Rimes
      • Season 5: Nick Lachey

    • In front of a live studio audience

      According to Insider, even though each episode is new to home viewers and the shouting audience on TV makes it appear as though the show is being recorded live, the performances are not truly taking place in real-time.

    • Who is Jordan Mailata?

      Jordan Mailata, born March 31, 1997, is an Australian-Samoan offensive lineman who plays for the Philadelphia Eagles of the National Football League (NFL).

      He formerly represented the Canterbury-Bankstown Bulldogs under-18s squad and the South Sydney Rabbitohs under-20s team in rugby league.

      In 2018, he became a member of the Eagles through the NFL's International Player Pathway Program.

    • Who is Jorge García?

      Born April 28, 1973, Jorge García is an actor and comedian from the United States.

      García originally gained notoriety for his role as Hector Lopez on the television show Becker, but he is most recognized for his role as Hugo "Hurley" Reyes in the television series Lost, which he played from 2004 until 2010.

      He portrayed a small part on ABC's Once Upon a Time and appeared in the Fox television series Alcatraz.

      García featured in the Netflix original movie The Ridiculous 6 and also played Jerry Ortega on Hawaii Five-0.

    • Who is Joe Buck?

      Joe Buck is an American sportscaster and the son of sportscaster Jack Buck. He was born on April 25, 1969.

      From 1994 until 2022, he worked for Fox Sports, notably as the network's principal play-by-play announcer for the National Football League and Major League Baseball coverage.

      With the exception of 1997 and 1999, when Bob Costas called those particular World Series for NBC, he was the play-by-play announcer for the World Series from 1996 to 2021.

      Buck is set to join ESPN as the principal play-by-play announcer for Monday Night Football in 2022.

    • Who is Rudy Giuliani?

      Rudy Giuliani, an American politician, and attorney was born on May 28, 1944, in New York, New York, and famously served as Mayor of New York City from 1994 to 2001.

      Giuliani was the US Associate Attorney General from 1981 to 1983 and the US Attorney for the Southern District of New York from 1983 to 1989 before becoming Mayor of New York.

      He is a Republican Party member who is most known for representing Donald Trump.

    • Who is Duff Goldman?

      Jeffrey Adam “Duff” Goldman is a pastry chef, a television personality, and the author of many cookbooks.

      He is the executive chef of two bakeries: Charm City Cakes in Baltimore, which was featured on Food Network’s reality show Ace of Cakes, and Charm City Cakes West in Los Angeles, which has been featured on Food Network’s Duff Till Dawn and “Cake Masters” series.

      Among the shows he’s hosted or contributed to are Food Network Challenge, Iron Chef America, Oprah, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, Man v. Food, Buddy vs. Duff, Duff Takes the Cake, and Duff’s Happy Fun Bake Time.
